Chonnam National University

Chonnam National University (CNU) is a public research university located in Gwangju, South Korea. The university was founded in 1952 as the flagship national university for the Jeollanam-do province and has since grown to become one of the leading universities in the country.
CNU offers undergraduate and graduate programs in various fields such as humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, engineering, medicine, and arts. The university has 18 colleges, 72 departments, and 11 graduate schools.
The campus is spread over two locations: Gwangju and Yeosu. The Gwangju campus is the main campus and is located in the city center, while the Yeosu campus is situated on the coast and focuses on marine science and technology.
CNU is known for its strong research programs and has partnerships with various institutions and organizations around the world. The university has several research centers and institutes that focus on areas such as nanotechnology, biotechnology, renewable energy, and Korean studies.
The university has a diverse student body, with approximately 35,000 students, including international students from over 100 countries. The campus has various facilities for students, including libraries, sports facilities, and student clubs and organizations.
Overall, Chonnam National University is a well-respected institution in South Korea with a strong academic reputation and a commitment to research and innovation.
